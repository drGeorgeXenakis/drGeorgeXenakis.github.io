Key publications

Soil-Plant-Atmosphere Continuum Hydraulic Efficiency

This is a new methodology for monitoring water deficit from continuous measurements and at different scales. The method is based on the Electric Circuit Theory. You can find more details in the preprint (currently submitted in New Phytologist).

Xenakis, G., 2023. Electric Circuit Theory as a method for monitoring tree water deficit at different scales. EarthArXiv Preprint, https://doi.org/10.31223/X54T19

There is also an Rshiny app demonstrating the application of the new methdology as an index of tree water deficit. Click on the link for the TWDM (Tree Water Deficit Monitoring) app.

Harwood Forest flux site

We run two flux sites at Harwood Forest, Northumberland. One mature (no longer operation) and one young (planted in 2017). You can find a detailed description of the site, measurements and results in the link below

Xenakis, G., Ash, A., Siebicke,s L., Perks, M., Morison, J.I.L. 2021. Comparison of the carbon, water, and energy balances of mature stand and clear-fell stages in a British Sitka spruce forest and the impact of the 2018 drought. Agricultural and Forest Meteorology, Volume 306, 108437 https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agrformet.2021.108437
